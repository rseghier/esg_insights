#Rize Tackles #Methane with #Innovation in Rice Farming
Key Takeaways:
- Adoption of alternate wetting and drying reduces methane emissions by 50%.
- Incentives for farmers include lower input costs, driving a 98% retention rate.
- Expansion and carbon credits aim to scale impact and profits.

Further Insights
1. Methane Challenge:
Rice cultivation, particularly in flooded fields, contributes to 10% of global methane emissions, a potent greenhouse gas.

2. Rize's Strategy:
Singapore-based startup Rize facilitates the use of alternate wetting and drying in rice farming to halve methane emissions, offering farmers cheaper seeds and supplies as an incentive.

3. Funding and Expansion:
With $14 million in Series A funding led by entities like Breakthrough Energy Ventures, Rize aims to expand its reach in Southeast Asia and enhance its business model with carbon credit sales.
