China's Emission Peak? #China #ClimateChange #CarbonBrief
Key Takeaways:
- China's CO2 emissions rose to 12.6bn tonnes in 2023.
- First emissions decline in 14 months observed in March.
- Preliminary data indicates continued fall in China's emissions.

Further Insights
1. Emission Surge:
Post-COVID recovery led to a 4.7% increase in China's carbon-dioxide emissions, reaching a record 12.6bn tonnes.

2. Recent Decline:
March saw a 3% year-on-year drop in emissions, marking the first decline after consistent increases for over a year.

3. Continued Drop:
Preliminary data suggests emissions also fell in April, indicating a potential trend.
