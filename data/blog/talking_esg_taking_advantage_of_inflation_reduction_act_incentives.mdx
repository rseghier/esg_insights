Impacts of #InflationReductionAct on #SustainableEnergy
Key Takeaways:
- Quality and detailed emission stories are essential for accessing green incentives.
- New guidance unlocks #SustainableAviationFuel opportunities, enhancing the aviation industry's green shift.
- Electric vehicle credits are subject to stringent supply chain criteria, impacting eligibility.

Further Insights
1. IRA Clarifications:
Recent guidance from the Treasury Department clarified the utilization of green incentives in the Inflation Reduction Act, crucial for sustainable project planning.

2. Workforce Requirements:
Prevailing wage and apprenticeship requirements detailed, indicating projects starting after January 2023 must monitor compliance closely to maximize credit benefits.

3. Tax Credit Transferability:
The introduction of transferability for some energy credits offers new financial structuring opportunities for projects and investors.

4. EV Regulation Adjustments:
Final electric vehicle regulations impose restrictions on vehicles eligible for full credit based on supply chain sourcing, significantly limiting qualified models.

5. SAF Model Revision:
Adjustments to the GREET model for sustainable aviation fuel by the DOE aligns with the Inflation Reduction Act's policy intentions, promoting industry compliance.

6. Energy Manufacturing Boost:
Round 2 of the 48c program for advanced energy manufacturing projects opens, promising substantial tax credits and emphasizing job creation.

Closing Thoughts:
With the ongoing release of regulatory guidance, how well-prepared is your business to leverage these changes for environmental and financial benefits?
