#EU Incentives, #FSR Impact, and #GreenDeals Unpacked
Key Takeaways:
- Navigating EU incentives requires urgent data collection on foreign subsidies.
- EU's FSR aims to level the playing field, not just disadvantage non-EU firms.
- Expertise is key to leveraging #GreenDeal funds and navigating complex regulations.

Further Insights
1. EU Shift:
The EU is focusing on becoming self-sustaining and resilient in energy through various subsidies and regulations, emphasizing the significance of expertise in implementing new frameworks.

2. Global Impact:
Global response to the US Inflation Reduction Act and similar legislation worldwide is shaping a race for future carbon-neutral technologies and economic resilience.

3. Foreign Subsidies Regulation:
FSR extends EU state aid rules globally, affecting companies with foreign subsidies, emphasizing the need for transparency and compliance to operate within the EU.

4. Incentives Awareness:
Most organizations are unaware of the details within the EU's Green Deal, missing out on potential incentives for sustainability projects.

5. Skills Gap:
A significant challenge for companies is the lack of expertise to comply with new laws and capitalize on incentives, indicating a need for trained professionals.

Closing Thoughts:
How prepared are businesses to adapt to this rapidly evolving regulatory landscape, and is there enough cross-sector collaboration to meet these ambitious sustainability goals?
