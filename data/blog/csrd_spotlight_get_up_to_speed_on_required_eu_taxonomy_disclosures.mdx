#CSRD Explained: EU #Taxonomy, #SustainabilityReporting
Key Takeaways:
- EU Taxonomy under CSRD requires detailed sustainability reporting.
- Identifying sustainable activities is crucial for reporting.
- Preparation involves complex criteria beyond basic KPIs.

Further Insights
1. Introduction:
PwC discusses the importance of delving into the EU's Taxonomy for sustainable activities under CSRD, advising companies to understand their impact.

2. EU Goals:
The EU Taxonomy is driven by goals of the European Green Deal aiming for climate neutrality by 2050, requiring a shift in corporate behaviors towards sustainability.

3. Scope and Applicability:
Not strictly defined, but closely aligned with CSRD requirements, implying companies reporting under CSRD should investigate their taxonomy applicability.

4. Six Environmental Objectives:
Focuses on climate change mitigation/adaptation, water, circular economy, pollution prevention, and biodiversity, outlining activities contributing to these objectives.

5. Technical Screening Criteria:
Activities must contribute substantially to one objective without harming others, supported by delegated acts that detail specific criteria and KPIs for reporting.

6. Reportable KPIs:
Includes green turnover, capex, and opex, focusing on the portion of activities aligned with taxonomy objectives relative to total activities.

Closing Thoughts:
As companies navigate the complexity of the EU Taxonomy, how can they best align their sustainability efforts for transparent reporting and leveraging financial sector influence?
